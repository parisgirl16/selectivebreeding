BACKGROUND of

SELECTIVE BREEDING

HISTORY


What is selective breeding?

Selective breeding is breeding two individuals with specific traits to increase the appearance of those traits in a population of a species.


When did the practice begin?

The practice of selective breeding began about 30,000 years ago in Eurasia with the domestication of dogs. Humans selectively bred wolves to exhibit certain traits to domesticate them.


What is the purpose of selectively breeding dogs?

Dogs are selectively bred to exhibit certain traits, like fur texture and color, body type, and temperament.


How many dog breeds exist today?

Around 400 dog breeds exist in the modern world.


Why is selective breeding harmful?

Excessive inbreeding for specific traits has led to a wide variety of genetic health issues in dogs. Some of those issues include respiratory problems, joint problems, and vision problems. Learn more about specific breeds and their genetic diseases here.


What are some alternatives to buying selectively bred dogs from puppy mill breeders?

"Adopt. Don't Shop." Instead of purchasing a dog from a puppy mill or unethical breeder, adopt or rescue a dog from a shelter. Many shelters are overflowing with animals and desperately need help. You can find some local shelters here.
